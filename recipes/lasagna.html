Lasagna

This classic lasagna recipe features layers of rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and perfectly cooked pasta sheets, all topped with a generous layer of melted mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. The hearty meat sauce is made by simmering ground beef and Italian sausage with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and herbs until thick and flavorful. Between each layer of sauce, smooth béchamel adds a touch of elegance, making every bite creamy and comforting. Once assembled, the lasagna is baked until bubbly and golden brown, filling the kitchen with an irresistible aroma. Perfect for family dinners or special occasions, this lasagna is a comforting dish that's sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 9 lasagna noodles (or no-boil noodles)
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 oz) pasta sauce (any kind you like)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Cook the noodles (if not using no-boil). Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook the meat: In a pan, cook ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any fat. Add pasta sauce and simmer for 5–10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Spread a little sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Add a layer of noodles. Spread some ricotta, meat sauce, and mozzarella. Repeat layers (usually 3 times). Finish with sauce and top with mozzarella and Parmesan.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30–40 minutes, until hot and bubbly.
  5. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes before serving.
